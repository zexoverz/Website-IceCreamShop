Gelato

Italian gelato ripples and forms imperfect scoops because it is so much denser than standard American ice cream. That's because it contains a higher volume of milk than cream.

Italian Ice

Often confused with American sherbet, Italian ice — AKA "water ice" — is fruit-based and made by the same process as standard ice cream (freezing the ingredients while mixing them).

Sorbet

There's no dairy in this fruit-forward frozen treat, making it denser and much richer in taste than traditional ice cream. Sometimes there's even alcohol in the recipe to lower the freezing.
